action adventure

KEYWORDS

puzzle, scientific method, mechanics, geometry, heavy weapons, tank, girl

SIMILAR TO OTHER GAMES?

aki's quest takes ideas from many places:
  • blaster master (tank, and person that can get out, weapons powerup)
  • ankh (triggerable objects, puzzles, remote control room)
  • quake (heavy weapons, rocket, grenade, railgun, nailgun)
  • zelda (market places you can buy and sell at)
  • super mario (jumpsquash, size powerups (in this game size wont change))
  • mario 64 (3rd person camera action)
  • return to krondor, diablo (inventory screen, equip your character)
  • lode runner (digging to trap enemies)
  • duke nukem (tripwire, pipebomb)
  • all rpg games (player stats)

basically blastermaster exploration, ankh puzzles/philosophy, quake weaponry, with zelda adventure, and mario/zelda fantasy.

OBJECTIVE

general: Save the universe while trying to get back home. specific: Solve a series of puzzles using scientific method to advance in the game. Using heavy weapons, defeating any enemy that gets in the way of the adventure. Find and gather clues about getting your home portal to open again, and possibly save the universe

WHY THIS GAME IS FUN

player needs to be clever (puzzles!), lots of weapons, powerups, character building, RPG, puzzle, and arcade action combined

throughout, the player achievs a feeling of discovery (very important)
the player can express themselvs in many ways at once or one at a time

  • engineering/problemsolving
  • skill/weaponry
  • budgeting of wealth and powerups.

This game will also appeal to those who enjoy ancient civilization + technology + magic (tech = mechanical, electricity, atomic)

TARGET AUDIENCE

people who like puzzles with a mechanical/scientific slant combined with adventure with plenty of action complimented by an intelligent reason for playing (not stupid/weak like "save the princess"). The game will start out easy so anyone can play, and get progressivly harder.

GAMEPLAY IN GENERAL

character

    All realtime 3rd person character control, like mario 64, zelda 64. This game is not turnbased! (your character always has freedom of movement and actions).

    Main character is a girl named Aki. Aki is a normal girl who unwittingly gets thrown into a quest. With the help of some high tech vehicles and heavy weapons she can really kick some ass (secondary action).

    You control Aki, or one of her vehicles depending on where they are needed in the game. And sometimes you use Aki to press buttons on a control panel to control some other thing (like a robot)...

puzzles

    Along the way are several intriguing puzzles (primary action) designed to make the player think. Each world has is designed for exploration and the puzzles it contains. No bosses. There are major and minor puzzles. There are enemies along the way to slow you down or kill you (and excuse to play with some impressivly flashy weaponry - rocket launchers, plasma, etc...). Friendly NPCs and Solving puzzles can give you small clues about your mysterious quest. Killing enemies can give you wealth.

    Puzzles are located below ground. This is because below ground is a tricky place created from the energy that connects all worlds. To navigate through this subterrania, you must get through puzzles. To solve the puzzles you use scientific method (see puzzle solving guide). If player gets stuck there will be an oracle hidden above ground. There will/may be clues to help the player find this oracle above ground or below. The oracle is not nessesarily a person though. It doesn't give a direct answer. Using some animated geometry it will show you something intended to spark your imagination as to how to solve the puzzle. Similarly there will be a map room hidden in each above land area to show you (with images) where you've been.

    The game is structured in medium size levels (like ankh, zelda, blaster master). There is a mechanical/lego/engineering puzzle element. Objects will have many facets ("use" makes a sound, while shooting it redirects the bullet). and there are enemies to block your way. Kill enemies with weapons you find along the way, ammo is limited also so you need to find that too. very seldom you will also find a storefront where you can trade sell or buy. your life is limited by your health, and you have only one life. saving is done with powercrystals found at checkpoints along the game. you are allowed a save at exit as well. you can accumulate wealth. every x amount of wealth gives you some health. You can find health powerups too. There will be some places that your character can't go (because a halway is too narrow etc..) so you will have to get out of your ship. The ship and the player can be upgraded in terms of ship body, and player armor.

STORY / PREMISE

The world of Aki's Quest is balanced by two eternally opposing forces: the good and the evil. (Names need to be made for the forces) The good side governs the above ground, and the evil side governs the below ground area. Both sides are necessary for the planet's survival, and each side is necessary for the existance of the other (Without the good side, the evil side couldn't exist, and vice versa), the below ground areas are connected with the above ground areas, but the good priests cannot go below ground, and the evil priests cannot travel above ground. The good and the evil have been at war with each other ever since the beginning of the planet's existance.

We haven't decided upon how to implement the "powers" of both the good and evil forces. The different alternatives are discussed below.)

Elemental System

They fight each other using the elements native to the planet; to strengthen their magical powers, they must develop the natural setting of the planet into something complicated. (Make one tree a forest, make an empty desert have an oasis, etc. for good, and evil must make a cave an underground maze, change a simple tunnel into a three mile passageway, and so on). Because they must develop their powers through this process, the war has caused the creation and development of all terrain, landscape, and life (excluding the humans) on both the above ground and below ground areas. By developing this landscape, the good side obtains the following elements:

  • Light
  • Fire
  • Water
  • Wind
  • Green (life of trees, grass, etc)

By developing the subterranean, the evil side obtains the following elements:

  • Shadow
  • Flare
  • Water
  • Earth
  • Miasma

For thousands of years the two sides have battled to a stalemate, and the balance of the planet was maintained. Priests of the evil were born to their elements, and priests of the good were born to theirs as well. Both orders did not realize that they were connected through two of the elements (flare connects to fire, and water connects to water).

Until, a man born of shadow, flare, and water met a woman born of light, fire, and water. The shadow made the man into a sinister sort of attractiveness (jet black/blue hair, dark eyes, grey skin, but muscular figure, etc.) The flare gave the man's inner passions strength, and the water made him cool, calm, and composed on the outside.

The light made the woman look like a golden haird celestial being; the fire made her impulsive, temperamental, and passionate about everything. The water served to keep a rein on her inner thoughts, making her personality very flexible and adaptable to any situation (In other words, she wouldn't panic on the inside).

The man was a genius, and consequently, the most powerful evil priest to be born. He was very eccentric, and defied what he refered to as the "traditional" way of doing things. Because of this, he was isolated from any of his peers, and spent most of his time exploring/developing his power in different ways. This is how he discovered the link in the elements. He used that link to view the world above ground, and eventually, he came upon the woman.

The man and the woman met, and the flare fed the man's infatuation with her beauty, and the fire fed the woman's impulses. The man couldn't resist the belief that she was the most beautiful woman and was just for him, and the woman couldn't resist her impulse to pursue the man.

Engineering System

Instead of magic, the two opposing factions are separated in a modernity vs. romantic lifestyle clash. The good priests lead lives that resemble those of Far Eastern monks. They derive their power through meditation and the development of the mind and body. Their architecture also reflects this lifestyle; the buildings will resemble Turkey in the late 1500's, or Japan in the 1500's, or the traditional Chinese architecture, or the traditional Native American architecture (Mayans, Incans, etc.), and perhaps the fantastic architecture of the elves. The good priests' power should also include the ability to tap into thought and action through a spoken word. These words are from an ancient language that is the binary language of all thought (~cough cough~ Snow Crash ~cough cough~).

The evil priests represent the modernity half of this clash, and they are completely technological. Their underground cities contain completely futuristic technology, and they have a utopian style of architecture. Huge buildings, domes, and lots of little robot helpers. Large intricate tracks and tunnels for futuristic vehicles, etc. Their weaponry is futuristic as well; beam guns, electron weaponry, etc. The priests themselves are more scientists than priests. The entire culture of the underground revolves around science, research and development.

General Outline of Past

Over the next year and a half, they courted, fell in love, married in secret (defying both orders and the balance of the planet), and had one child: a girl. The side of the man's family (those from the shadow element), found out, and they followed him one day to the above ground using the same bridge through flare and water that allowed him to meet the woman. Once above ground, the man's family immediately attacked the good priesthood using both the evil elements, and the two good elements (fire and water). The good priesthood, still unaware of the bridge between fire and water (and thus unable to draw upon it's power) were unable to push them back.

The woman, unaware of the man's innocence, thought her husband had betrayed her, and fled with the girl (who was still a baby). The woman, knowing of the bridge between fire and water, used light to hide herself and the child.

The man, refusing to aid his relatives in their conquest, instead searched for the woman during the war to try and explain things, but he was unable to locate her (due to the woman using light).

After several years of battle, the good priesthood was defeated and enslaved by the evil priesthood. The evil priesthood drew upon their powers and sealed the good priesthood below ground by creating intricate and complicated structures the likes of which had never been seen before. (Enter the puzzles.)

The woman, had literally drained all of her life energy by running and hiding both her and the child throughout the duration of the war. She staggered into a village (or whatever; this should be the place Aki starts out in the game) one night while the girl was sleeping; she left the child at someone's doorstep (haven't decided who) and walked into a lake near the village in order to perish without her body being discovered postmortem.

The man, finally gave up his search for his wife and child at the end of the war. Realizing what his family had done to the good priesthood, he used slightly manipulated the puzzles to allow for one of the good priest's to be freed without being noticed: His father-in-law. He entrusted his father-in-law with the tast of searching for the girl so that she could free the priests by destroying the puzzles, and restore the balance to the universe.

The evil priests, unable to return home, used their flare and water to disguise themselves as the good priesthood (who are actually enslaved) to the rest of the world. They then set out to finding a way to bridge the two worlds again, so that they might create one empire out of both dimensions.

The good priest disguised himself as an evil priest and joined their order so that he would be better equipped to find his granddaughter. One day, he hears that the evil priesthood has found a girl that might accomplish their goals, her name is Aki, and she is in a small village named Solace...

General Flow of Plot

Aki has lived a normal childhood in the village of Solace. Her parents found her at their doorstep one morning wrapped in a blanket. She was but a young child then. She's had a normal childhood in the small village (play with the dog, run around in the back yard, etc.) She has no memory of her life before the village, and she does not know of any other parents other than those in the village. As the game progresses, the player will learn more of Aki's past through flashbacks, stories told by different people, and written pieces in game. She will first discover that she is NOT a normal girl, and gradually, she will learn of her parents and how they met. This will continue until she learns first of her mother's death. Finally, she will learn about her father's sacrifice, and this will allow her to travel to the evil priests' domain.

PUZZLE DESIGNING GUIDE.

puzzles are solved using the scientific method:

  • observe and examine and explore
  • collect facts from environment and from your inventory
  • apply these facts to the problem/puzzle at hand
Puzzles are never solved by chance

Be clever, these puzzles should be logic based, make you think, and make you appreciate it when you've solved it (i.e. it shouldn't be obvious). The puzzles can be imbedded into the map as well (below ground generally) throughout 2 or more rooms, or small puzzles in just one room. Generally I think of puzzles as hunt/gather/apply - where you need to find the pieces, then transport them to the problem area, then figure out how to assemble them to solve the problem. use your engineering minds!

Most puzzles will require some object (key, block, crystal, mechanism, spring, etc...)

  • materials should be interesting, for example:
    • audio upon [use] or when shot
    • redirection of bullet when shot
    • makes game objects appear or dissapear when held in inventory
    • increase size of inventory, when dropped will eject any items exceeding your inventory
  • through observation and experimentation (only) the player will learn the object capabilities and learn how to aplpy the tools to problems found throughout the game.

OUR STORY SO FAR

Main character is a girl named Aki. Aki is a normal girl, likes to play etc... she is especially facinated with toy guns and rocket launchers, and spends many hours in the back yard with her dog.

One night she wakes up to the sound of scratching at her closet. Fearless, she gets up to investigate and discovers that there is a cool wind coming from under the door. Throwing it open she climbs in and emerges into a rocky cavernlike tunnel. The door slams shut behind her and she is unable to open it.

An old man steps out of the shadows and introduces himself as a good priest. He seems delighted to have found her. He claims to need her help.

"What for?" Aki questions.

"The world around us is in trouble, Aki. There is an evil underground that seeks to conquer our land and its way of life. We good priests are all that remain to guard this land."

((Note: The good priest is refraining from telling Aki of their enslavement. He doesn't want to risk alerting the evil priests.))

"How can I possibly help one of your kind with such a task?"

"Our power is waning Aki. We can only use the simplest versions of what we used to be capable of. We have tried to stop the evil underground, but we are not strong enough. Those that have gone below have not returned. For years, we have searched for someone with the ability to travel underground without aid. Your entrance into this tunnel proves it; you ARE the chosen one."

"What do you mean?" Aki asks, puzzled. "This is just a tunnel below my house."

The old man smirks. "It took me three days of meditation just to enter this particular tunnel. I won't tell you how long it took me to actually travel through it. You, my dear, just accomplished such a feat in a matter of seconds."

Aki jumps back, astonished. She was, however sympathetic, for he seemed like such a nice old man. "What can I do to help?"

"I'm afraid all I can tell you is to explore the tunnels. As no one who has gone below has actually returned, I cannot fathom what you will encounter. However, I might be able to arrange for some small aid."

((Note: He gives her the tank?))

"All right, I will travel through the underground, but I still don't know if I can be of any help to you."

Aki travels through the tunnel to the end, and emerges in the city of Atla.

VILLAGE OF ATLA (THEME IS ANCIENT MAYAN)

At the base of the ziggurat there is a marketplace. Unsure what to do, Aki tries to blend in and checks out several of the stalls. One of them houses a strange mystic that upon seeing her invites her to come in and talk. Asking him questions teaches her much about her new world. Strangely he knows about her entrance from the closet, and informs her that she will not be able to get back because there is a force holding it closed. To disable the force will require some research and aquisition of powers the mystic can not provide. Thus he suggests that she explore the land and see what she can find. throughout the market are hidden powerups, so exploring here is a good thing. NOTEs: she doesn't find her vehicle until later, inner and outer atla she is walking and building her character Zelda 64 style.

OUTER ATLA (JUNGLE WITH CLEARINGS)

Here powerups are found in organic containers: mushroom stools, on enemies, etc... Also found in plain view: floating items, gems. (expand on this, gameplay, enemies, puzzles, etc...) Hidden is an entrance to the subterrainian world.

SUBTERRAIN ATLA (ROCKY + TECHNOLOGY)

lots of loderunner and mario action (ledges, weapon/item/wealth powerups, enemies). Here is where she vinds a vehicle, appanently it was being stored by some techomancer... To find it she solved 3 puzzles involving locks. The locks are not standard locks, but must be cleverly triggered. Inspection of the world are all the hints she has.

1st puzzle

10 rooms. press wall plates in a long winding corridor. The number above the plate opens the door with that number. you as a player need to notice that the numbers may be connected with each other.

2nd puzzle

she finds a key that emits a short tune. match this to the door that emits the same tune.

3rd puzzle

she finds a new kind of object that when "used" it emits a burst of energy (or bullet)... The burst always exits from the same direction (world aligned) no matter how she drops the item on the ground. She can "use" it while it is on the ground, or while it is in her inventory. Shooting it with a weapon will redirect the burst in the object's direction as well (only with certain gun). There will be a timed door that when triggered will close again after 2 seconds. Unfortunately for Aki the trigger is just too far away. She must place the shooting object near the trigger and shoot at it while standing near the door.

The vehicle is too big to fit through the way she came, and must exit out a larger tunnel, which leads her to a new world.... Plans on the walls of this cave showed that the vehicle is a prototype design. The plans illustrate the vehicle's advanced capabilities. currently, the vehicle can do the normal driving plus straef and jump. she soon notices that the vehicle is upgradable when she finds a simple weapon for it (drive over it to pick it up).

NOTE: the addition of the vehicle adds the need to find power for it. If the vehicle power runs out, then Aki's health meter is displayed, but will probably run out fast if against a vehicle-strength enemy... If vehicle power becomes too low, it will sink to the ground unable to move. Aki is able to get out and find vehicle power cells or repair kits.

Boxes

  1. Small Size: These boxes are up to Aki's knees. They can be stacked in the inventory (up to a certain number).
  2. Medium Size: These boxes are up to Aki's shoulders. She can move them, pick them up and carry them, but not place them in her inventory.
  3. Large Size: These are taller than Aki. She cannot pick these up, but she can still move them by pushing and pulling.
Boxonomy
Box Size Box Name Box Attributes
Medium Ladder Box These boxes contain poles that extend from one side only. Aki places these on the ground, and a pole will extend from the side. These are useful as ladders and elevators.
ALL Light Sensitive Box These boxes are light sensitive. They react to light tools, and light environmental light. The boxes will do something once the proper light is applied.
Small Musical Box These boxes will play a tune when used. They must be brought to another box or location that will react to this tune.
ALL Trigger Boxes These boxes come in sets that react to each other. They will resemble each other in some way, for example, perhaps they all have pieces of a pattern painted on one side, or perhaps their corners are jagged so that they fit together, or perhaps they make the same noise when shot. There is a lot of possibility for variety here.
ALL Gun Triggered Boxes These boxes react to gunfire from various weapons. There affect once shot will vary from box to box.
ALL Ricochet Boxes These boxes will redirect certain types of gunfire. These must be used by Aki to solve puzzles that involve triggers from gunfire.

world items (can't pick them up)

  • Panel that when "use"d generates a trigger event. This can be hooked up to a receiving (personal or world) item.

just a raw brain dump...
- vehicle and aki mode (i.e. she can get in and out of the vehicle)
- powerups (for ship and aki)
  - for vehicle weapons, and aki weapons
  - health, speed, armor, etc..
  - treasure (different values of jewels spread everywhere like in Spiro)
  - aki can't carry ship powerups
  - ship can't carry aki powerups
- "get out of your vehicle" mode
  - there will be narrow areas that require this
  - there are a different class of weapons powerups for this mode
- Jump for both vehicle and aki
- puzzles
  - remote control
- RPG stats (for ship and aki)
  - speed 
  - aiming
  - armor
  - hand to hand combat
- bonus levels
- random thoughts
  - some enemies you can't squish
  - some you can't bury
  - some you can't shoot with certain weapons
- weapons
  - hot keys (like quake 1-9 and 0)
  - dig holes (like loderunner)
  - jumpsquash (supermario style)
  - traditional
    - rocket ...................(best)
    - railgun
    - chaingun (plasma?)
    - plasma ...................(worst)
- singleplayer with option for mplayer (pretty much singleplayer game)
- inventory screen
  - arrange picked up items.
  - specify what parts of ship has armor, weapons
  - covers entire view (to simulate not paying attention when arranging)
  - does not stop gameplay! :)
- triggered items
  - some remote control weapon (box that shoots, like in ankh)
    - the remote control can be picked up separately of course. :)
  - timed door who's trigger is in another room 
    (thus you'd have to use the remote control 
     weapon to push the button for you)
- savegame
  - save crystals found at every checkpoint give you the opportunity 
    to save them up and choose when they are used.
  - save is allowed at game exit (we're not cruel)
  - cheat mode gives a quick save anytime, but you don't get treasure.
  - consider this... quicksave available everywhere except for in areas 
    near the end of the level.
- lives
  - just one (like diablo ii)
  - you have a health meter (power ups will be available)
- vehicles
  - after certain "special" enemies are killed, you can repair their ship
    - and use it.
  - issue... how to repair the ship?
    - aki carries a repair kit found in special aki-only areas of the map
  - need to switch vehicles
    - new tank allows you to jump higher to a place you need to get to
    - more inventory but slower ship (or less and faster)
    - tank weapons can't transfer (cause the aki can't carry them!)
- camera (like mario64)
- enemies
  - when you learn the puzzles, the this can give the game replay
  - makes the game more fun, harder when puzzles are easy.
  - some can be buried, some jumpsquashed.  most can be shot, but not some. :)
